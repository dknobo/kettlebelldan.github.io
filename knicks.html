THE 2026 RUN

This wasn't supposed to happen. Not according to the experts. Not after decades of near-misses, injuries, and "almosts." But this group — led by Jalen Brunson, Josh Hart, and the rest of the core — refused to let history repeat itself.

They didn't just make the playoffs. They took the East. And in the process, they gave New York the first taste of real hope since the late 90s. The Garden is shaking again.

ECHOES FROM THE PAST

1994

THE EWING ERA

Patrick Ewing. John Starks. Charles Oakley. Anthony Mason.

The toughest, grittiest Knicks team in modern history. They went to the Finals and took the Houston Rockets to seven games. They played with a mean streak the league hadn't seen in years. New York fell in love with that physical, defensive identity.

They were one game away from a title. We still talk about Starks' 3s and Oak's elbows.

1999

THE 8-SEED MIRACLE

Latrell Sprewell. Allan Houston. Larry Johnson.

An 8-seed that somehow made it all the way to the Finals. The famous "4-point play." The improbable run that had the entire city believing again. They ran into the Spurs and Tim Duncan, but the journey felt like destiny for a few magical weeks.

1999 was the last time the Knicks reached the promised land. Until now.

The Garden. The roar. The history. This is what we're fighting for again.
